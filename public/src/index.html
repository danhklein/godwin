Hitler in America

What we talk about when we talk about Nazis



In 1990 Mike Godwin purposefully created one of the first internet memes. It contained no cats, but instead a single terse statement: “As an online discussion continues, the probability of a reference or comparison to Hitler or Nazis approaches 1.”

Godwin's Law brought into the information age a discussion that had been going on for half a century. What are we doing when we compare someone or their beliefs to the Nazis? Were we simplifying, dehumanizing, enraging? Was comparing something to the Holocaust diminishing or illuminating?
